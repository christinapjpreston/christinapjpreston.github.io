Salmonâ€™s model links online learning to a number of stages or steps required for happy and successful online learning:

 

  1. Access and motivation
  2. Online socialization
  3. Information exchange
  4. Knowledge Construction
  5. Development

 

For Salmon, online learning is a process by which the participant (student) moves through each stage of the model from step 1-5.Â  Each stage of the model requires the participant to develop and master different technical skills.Â  These technical skills at each stage are:

 

  1. Setting up systems and accessing
  2. Sending/receiving messages
  3. Searching, personalizing software.
  4. Conferencing
  5. Proving links outside closed conferences

 

A further element of her theory is the idea that participants in online learning need to be supported by an emoderator through a structured developmental process which reflects stages 1-5 of the model.Â  Salmonâ€™s model suggests that the emoderator is crucial to the online learning process and that each stage requires the exercise of different emoderating skills in order to support the online learning of the participant.

 

Emoderating skills required at each learning stage of the participant are:

 

  1. Welcoming and encouraging

 

 

  1. Familiarising/building bridges between cultural,social and learning environments
  2. Facilitating tasks and supporting use of learning materials
  3. Facilitating process
  4. Supporting, responding

 

Salmonâ€™s model also contains a bar which predicts that the amount of interactivity between participants at each stage of the model.Â  The 5-step model suggests that interactivity between participants will gradually increase in intensity as they pass through each stage of the model, reaching a peak as it participants grow closer to the top.Â  However Salmon does note that stage 5 â€˜often results in a return to more individual pursuits(2).

 

 

To some extent, Salmonâ€™s model can be related to our on-line community at the web-based Mirandanet.

 

Just as in Salmonâ€™s 5-step model, participants in the emoderation course at Mirandanet could be said to move through a logical process with carefully structured tasks to support learning.Â Â  Participants in the course have been motivated to access online discussions by the expectation that they would be successful in becoming emoderators. The course has also provided opportunities to interact with experienced emoderaters.Â  As members ofÂ  theÂ  online community at Mirandanet, participants go through a process of induction into the software of the online discussion, being welcomed and encouraged to welcome otherÂ  participants to the online forums, before developing deeper level interactions (such as supporting, responding toÂ  contributions to the online discussion at Mirandanet).

For me, a wholistic approach to learning works best –Â  often the most effective approach but not always possible to explain in terms of a single theory.Â Â  A gradual movement around a learning curve (which in itself forms part of a larger circle)rather then a movement from step to step is perhaps a moreÂ  accurate description of my own learning style.Â  For me, then, Salmonâ€™s model has some relevance but is also simplistic and rigid, posing a number of contradictions.
